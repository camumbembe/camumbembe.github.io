Take it to the bridgeâ€¦ the Brooklyn Bridge

Arguably the most famous bridge in the world, the Brooklyn Bridge is not only aesthetically pleasing, it makes for a great walk. The 271-foot-tall neo-gothic arches are certainly the bridge's trademark, but strolling along the elevated pedestrian walkway is a must for any visitor.The bridge's construction was overseen first by John Augustus Roebling and then his son, Washington. But after Washington suddenly died, his wife, Emily Warren Roebling, took over and finished the job, a fact that has been largely lost to history.

Take a closer look at the Chrysler Building

Look closely enough and you'll notice the grills of automobiles at the top. But there are more than just car parts going on here: gargoyles jut out at floor 59 and eagles two floors above that. Most of the exterior of the building is off limits, but pop into the lobby, open to the public during the day, to get a taste of the marble-clad Art Deco interiors.

Explore Central Park and enjoy its many elements

Central Park is the work of genius landscape architect Frederick Law Olmsted. Olmsted's objective was to give city dwellers a reprieve from the blights of urban life, to make them forget that they were in one of the most bustling cities on the planet. It worked. Even today, one finds New Yorkers and New York visitors taking advantage of the park's many elements.
